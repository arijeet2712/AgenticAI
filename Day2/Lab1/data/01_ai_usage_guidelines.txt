AI Usage Guidelines (Internal Knowledge Base Excerpt)
Version: 1.3 | Last updated: 2025-09-18

Purpose
These guidelines describe how teams may use AI tools for drafting, summarization, coding assistance, and analytics,
while maintaining security, privacy, and quality standards.

Key principles
1) Data classification
- Public: may be used freely.
- Internal: may be used only with approved AI services and access controls.
- Confidential/Restricted: must not be sent to external services unless explicitly approved.

2) Prompt hygiene
- Do not include secrets, API keys, passwords, or private customer identifiers in prompts.
- Use placeholders (e.g., <API_KEY>, <CUSTOMER_ID>) and secure configuration stores.

3) Human review
- AI outputs must be reviewed for accuracy, bias, and completeness before use in production materials.
- Claims must be validated against source documents when the content is used for compliance, legal, or finance topics.

4) Logging and traceability
- Where feasible, store the user question, retrieval sources, and final answer for auditability.
- Maintain citation-style references (document id + section/chunk id) for grounded answers.

Examples of acceptable use
- Drafting email templates and internal communications
- Summarizing internal documentation that is already approved for internal sharing
- Generating unit-test scaffolding and refactoring suggestions (with review)

Examples of prohibited use
- Submitting private customer data without approval
- Using AI output as a final decision for hiring, compensation, or disciplinary action
